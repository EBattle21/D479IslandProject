About The City

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano. Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. The best time to visit Taniti is between April and June, when the weather is warm, the humidity is lower, and the island is alive with cultural festivals and fewer tourist crowds.
