Tourist Activities!

Restaurants: Taniti currently has 10 restaurants: five serve mostly local fish and rice, three serve American-style meals, and two serve Pan-Asian cuisine.

Stores: Taniti has two supermarkets, two smaller grocery stores, and one convenience store that is open 24 hours a day.

Taniti has a wide variety of lodging that ranges from an inexpensive hostel to one large, four-star resort. There are many small, family-owned hotels and a growing number of bed and breakfasts. All types of lodging are strictly regulated and regularly inspected by the Tanitian government.

Most people visit Taniti to enjoy the beaches, explore the rainforest, and to visit the volcano. However, there are other things to do, including visiting a local history museum, going on chartered fishing tours, snorkeling, zip-lining in the rainforest, visiting several pubs, including a microbrewery, dancing at a new dance club, seeing a movie, taking helicopter rides, playing at an arcade, visiting art galleries, and bowling. Also, a nine-hole golf course should be operational by next year. Many of these activities are located in Merriton Landing, which is a rapidly developing area on the north side of Yellow Leaf Bay.

Most tourists spend most of their time in Taniti City, which boasts native architecture and nearby white, sandy beaches that encircle Yellow Leaf Bay. Other popular activities include boat or bus tours of the island, hikes in the rainforest, or visits to Taniti‚Äôs active volcano.

Attraction Name: Aurelia by the Sea
Includes: Dinner, dessert, and two drinks at Taniti's favorite local restaurant. (Recommended Guests: 2)
A refined, seasonal tasting menu that highlights the best of land and sea. Techniques range from French and Japanese to Polynesian influences. Locally sourced seafood, tropical fruits, and fine imported ingredients are all prepared with subtle sophistication.


Attraction Name: Noire Isle
Includes: Contemporary French and Pacific fusion tasting menu, prix fixe only. A 7- to 11-course experience driven by seasonal ingredients, sustainable fishing, and artistic presentation. (Recommended Guests: 4)

Attraction Name: Turtle Time: Feed, Learn, and Paint
Includes: Meet rescued island turtles at a local conservation center. Kids and adults will help prepare food, hand-feed the turtles (where allowed), and learn about their care and rescue stories. The experience ends with a fun turtle-painting activity using eco-friendly paints and shells or canvas boards. (Recommended Guests: 3-6)

Attraction Name: Island Explorer Scavenger Hunt
Includes: Turn your island visit into an adventure with a guided scavenger hunt designed for families and curious kids! Follow clues across a safe, scenic beach trail to discover hidden animal tracks, native plants, sea shells, and secret landmarks. Includes a prize for completing the challenge and a refreshing treat at the end. (Recommended Guests: 3-6)
